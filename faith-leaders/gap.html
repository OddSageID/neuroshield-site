Principles Without Infrastructure

What Exists: The Rome Call for AI Ethics

The Vatican's Rome Call established crucial principles:

  • Transparency in algorithmic decision-making
  • Inclusion of vulnerable populations
  • Responsibility for AI impacts
  • Impartiality free from bias
  • Reliability of systems
  • Security and privacy protection

Signatories include: Microsoft, IBM, FAO, Italian government, and major faith institutions.

Achievement: Global consensus on ethical principles.

Limitation: No mechanism to operationalize these principles.

What Happened: The November 2025 Conference

The Vatican's conference on AI and Medicine demonstrated:

  • Ecumenical collaboration already exists
  • Shared concerns across faith traditions
  • Institutional willingness to engage
  • Recognition of urgency

Achievement: Dialogue and coordination among faith leaders.

Limitation: Conversation without implementation infrastructure.

The Current Reality for Ethical Technologists

Scenario

A senior AI researcher discovers their company's healthcare algorithm disproportionately denies care to vulnerable populations.

Ethical Response

Report the bias, advocate for correction.

Actual Consequence

  • Termination for "not being a team player"
  • Industry blacklisting
  • $150,000 in student debt, no income
  • Forced choice: Financial survival or ethical integrity

Result: The most ethically-minded technologists are systematically removed, leaving systems in the hands of those willing to compromise.

Only 9% of Americans always read privacy policies before agreeing (Pew Research, 2019)

The "click to agree" culture has automated away deliberation, normalizing passive consent to corporate control.

Who is building the systems that exploit this vulnerability? Not the technologists who were fired for raising concerns.
