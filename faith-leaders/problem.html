The Undefended Infrastructure

A Pattern of Vulnerability

Faith-based institutions have become high-value targets in an escalating cyber conflict, while their technologists who prioritize ethics face systematic displacement from the industry.

Incident Timeline

  1. July 2020 Vatican Cyberattack

    Sophisticated breach targeting Holy See networks. Attack vectors exploited legacy security architecture. Demonstrated urgent need for adaptive defense systems.

    Impact: Exposed vulnerability of institutions relying on outdated security models.

  2. 2020–2024 LDS/BYU Breaches

    Multiple incidents targeting Latter-day Saints educational and ecclesiastical systems. Patient data, student records, and institutional communications compromised. Traditional signature-based defenses failed against polymorphic threats.

    Impact: Confirmed pattern across faith traditions.

  3. 2025 Healthcare AI Authorization Crisis

    Unregulated AI systems generating prior authorization denials without human review. Denial rates 16 times higher than manual systems (American Medical Association, 2025). Physicians describe "automated denial at scale."

    Impact: Technology optimized for profit, not patient care.

  4. November 2025 Vatican Conference on AI and Medicine

    Global faith leaders convened to address AI ethics. Catholic, LDS, Orthodox, Protestant institutions demonstrated existing ecumenical collaboration. Confirmed alignment on principles.

    Gap Identified: No operational infrastructure to implement shared values.

The Human Cost

While institutions face digital threats, the technologists capable of defending them face a different crisis:

Displacement of Ethical Technologists

  • AI ethics team members laid off from major tech companies
  • Industry blacklisting for whistleblowing or refusing unethical projects
  • Significant student debt with no income pathway
  • Financial coercion forcing compromise of principles
  • No institutional sanctuary or protected employment

The Result: Those most qualified to build ethical systems are systematically removed from the field.

Only 9% of Americans always read privacy policies before agreeing (Pew Research, 2019)

The "click to agree" culture has automated away deliberation, normalizing passive consent to corporate control.

Who is building the systems that exploit this vulnerability? Not the technologists who were fired for raising concerns.
