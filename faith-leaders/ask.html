Partnership for Ethical Infrastructure

What We're Asking

Faith institutions can transform ethical principles into operational reality by:

  1. Financial Sponsorship
  2. Institutional Legitimacy
  3. Infrastructure Support
  4. Governance Participation

Sponsorship Tiers

Founding Sponsor — $5M over 5 years

Commitment

  • Primary funding for initial cohort (10 technologists)
  • Infrastructure and equipment costs
  • Governance board seat

Benefits

  • Priority access to developed systems
  • Dedicated cybersecurity support
  • Co-branding on research publications
  • First implementation of ethical AI systems
  • Legacy as founding institution

Sustaining Sponsor — $2M over 5 years

Commitment

  • Support for 4 technologists
  • Operational costs
  • Advisory board participation

Benefits

  • Access to developed systems
  • Cybersecurity consultation
  • Participation in research priorities
  • Implementation support

Supporting Sponsor — $500K over 5 years

Commitment

  • Support for 1 technologist
  • Program development

Benefits

  • System licensing at reduced cost
  • Quarterly briefings
  • Collaborative research opportunities

In-Kind Sponsorship

Risk Mitigation

What if the technologists underperform?

Performance metrics tied to continued employment, with probationary periods and peer review.

What if systems don't work as promised?

Iterative development with sponsor feedback, pilot testing, and staged deployment.

What's our liability if something goes wrong?

Shared governance structure distributes responsibility; insurance covers technical failures.

How do we ensure they're actually building ethical systems?

Transparent code repositories, external audits, governance board oversight, public accountability.
