For Technologists > The Call

You Were Cast Out for Refusing to Build the Cage

They Called It "Not Being a Team Player"

You raised concerns about bias in the algorithm. You questioned whether the model was optimizing for patient outcomes or profit margins. You asked if users truly understood what they were consenting to.

And for that, you were terminated.

They dressed it up in corporate language—"organizational restructuring," "shifting priorities," "cultural fit"—but you know the truth:

You were fired for choosing ethics over expedience.

The Weight You Carry

$150,000 in student debt.
Six years of education to become the kind of technologist who could build systems that actually help people.

No income.
Blacklisted from an industry that values compliance over conscience.

Financial coercion.
The weight of debt doesn't care about your principles. It demands you find work, any work, even if it means building exactly what you refused to build before.

The cage tightens.

You Are Not Alone

Across the industry, ethical AI researchers, cybersecurity specialists, and principled engineers face the same choice:

  • Compromise your values and keep your job
  • Stand firm and lose everything

Google's AI ethics team: Disbanded after raising concerns

Meta's responsible AI division: Gutted in layoffs

OpenAI's safety researchers: Departed after policy disagreements

Healthcare AI whistleblowers: Terminated, sued, silenced

The pattern is clear: Corporations systematically remove those who prioritize human dignity over profit.

What You Know That Others Don't

You've seen the code. You understand what behavioral AI can do when deployed without oversight:

  • Pre-conscious manipulation through interface design
  • Automated institutional decay in healthcare
  • Cognitive exploitation at infrastructure level
  • Erosion of human autonomy disguised as convenience

You know that the systems currently being built don't just influence behavior—they predict and preempt choice itself.

You know that without transparent, human-augmented oversight, AI becomes not a tool for empowerment but an instrument of control.

You know the difference between the shield and the cage.

And you refused to build the cage.

The Question You Face

How long can you hold out?

How many months before the debt collectors escalate?

How many rejections before you accept a position that requires you to compromise?

How many nights of anxiety before you give in?

Financial pressure is a weapon. It has been used against you with precision.

But there is another path.

"I spent three years building healthcare AI. When I discovered our authorization algorithm was denying care to dialysis patients at twice the rate of others, I reported it. I was escorted out within a week."

— Former Senior ML Engineer, Major Healthcare Tech Company

9%

of Americans always read privacy policies before agreeing (Pew Research, 2019)

Someone designed those policies to be unreadable. Someone built the interfaces that exploit this exhaustion. Someone created the systems that turn consent into automation.

It wasn't you. Because you refused.
