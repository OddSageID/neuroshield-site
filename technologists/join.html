Step 1: Expression of Interest

Not an application. Just a conversation.

We'll ask:

  • Your background and expertise
  • What ethical concerns led to your departure
  • What kind of systems you want to build
  • What questions you have about the Order

No commitment. No pressure. Just dialogue.

Timeline: We'll respond within one week to schedule a call.

Express Interest

Step 2: Exploratory Call

30-minute video conversation with Order leadership.

We'll discuss:

  • Your specific situation and needs
  • Whether the Order is the right fit
  • What projects align with your expertise
  • The application process if you want to proceed

This is mutual discernment. We're not just evaluating you—you're evaluating us.

Outcome: If both sides are interested, move to application.

Step 3: Formal Application

Materials Needed

  • Technical portfolio: Code samples, projects, publications
  • Professional references: 2–3 people who can attest to your ethics and competence
  • Personal statement: Why the Order, what you hope to build
  • Verification of ethical departure: Documentation of whistleblowing, termination for principled refusal, etc.

Review Process

  • Technical assessment: Can you actually build these systems?
  • Ethics verification: Do you have demonstrated track record?
  • Values alignment interview: Are we mission-compatible?

Timeline: 4–6 weeks from application to decision.

Step 4: Onboarding

If accepted:

Immediate

  • Debt relief agreement finalized (first payment within 60 days)
  • Employment contract signed
  • Benefits enrollment
  • Project assignment

First 90 Days

  • Intensive orientation on Rome Call principles
  • Technical training on Order systems and standards
  • Team integration
  • Probationary review (ensures mutual fit)

First Year

  • Full project ownership
  • Contribution to Order publications
  • Conference presentations
  • Ongoing professional development

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't have student debt?

Salary adjustment compensates, or you can direct "debt relief" funds to dependents' education.

I'm not religious. Is this a problem?

No. The Order operates under faith sponsorship but does not require members to hold religious belief. Respect for the mission and values is required; personal faith is not.

What if I want to leave before 5 years?

You can separate at any time. Debt relief received to that point is not clawed back, but future payments cease. No penalty beyond losing the benefit.

What if I'm offered a better-paying job elsewhere?

We hope you stay for the mission, not just the money. But we're also realistic—if a truly compelling opportunity arises, we can discuss transition.

How do I know this is real and not a scam?

Valid concern. We provide: verifiable sponsoring institution documentation, legal entity formation documents, financial transparency, references from governance board members, and video calls with leadership before any commitment.

What if the Order fails or loses funding?

Employment contracts include severance provisions. Any debt relief already provided is not reclaimed. We're building for sustainability, but we acknowledge the risk.

Can I work remotely?

Depends on project. Some work requires on-site presence at sponsor institutions. Other work can be remote. We'll discuss during exploratory call.

What's the catch?

The "catch" is this: You must actually build excellent ethical systems. Performance standards are high. This isn't a refuge for incompetence—it's a sanctuary for principled competence.
