Frequently Asked Questions

Questions from Both Audiences

For Potential Sponsors

Why should we fund this instead of hiring security consultants?

Consultants build temporary solutions optimized for billable hours. The Order builds permanent infrastructure aligned with your values, staffed by technologists you've liberated from corporate coercion.

Cost Comparison:

  • Major cybersecurity consulting firm: $500K–$2M/year, ongoing
  • The Order: $270K/year per technologist (including debt relief), permanent capability building

Value Difference:

  • Consultants: Profit-driven, temporary, black-box solutions
  • The Order: Mission-aligned, permanent, transparent systems
How do we ensure quality and accountability?

Three-layer governance:

  1. Technical: Peer review, code audits, performance metrics
  2. Ethical: External ethics board reviews, transparency reports
  3. Institutional: Sponsor governance board oversight

Plus: Probationary periods, renewable contracts, clear performance standards.

What's our liability if systems fail or cause harm?

Shared governance structure distributes responsibility appropriately:

  • Technical failures: Order maintains professional liability insurance
  • Deployment decisions: Sponsoring institution retains control
  • Ethical violations: Joint accountability with remediation protocols

Legal structure creates separation between Order operations and sponsor liability while maintaining oversight.

Can we terminate our sponsorship if we're not satisfied?

Yes. Sponsorship agreements include:

  • Annual review periods
  • 90-day notice for withdrawal
  • Transition plans for sponsored technologists
  • Prorated refunds for unused funds (if applicable)

We want committed sponsors, not reluctant ones.

How many sponsors are already committed?

Current status: Exploratory discussions with multiple institutions across Catholic, LDS, Orthodox, and Protestant traditions.

This is founding stage. Early sponsors have maximum influence on structure and direction.

For Potential Recruits

Is this just a non-profit with lower pay?

No. Salary + debt relief = competitive total compensation.

Comparison:

  • Big Tech: $150K salary + ethical compromise + risk of termination
  • The Order: $90K salary + $40K debt relief = $130K total + protected employment + meaningful work

Plus: When your debt is eliminated (3–5 years), your effective lifetime earnings are higher.

What if I don't want to commit to 5 years?

Initial commitment is 2 years. After that, annual renewals up to 10 years total.

You can leave at any time. Debt relief already received is not clawed back, but future payments stop.

I'm an atheist. Is that a problem?

No. The Order operates under faith sponsorship but does not require members to hold religious belief.

Required: Respect for the mission (ethical AI aligned with human dignity)

Not required: Personal religious faith

We have technologists across the belief spectrum, from devout to atheist, united by shared values about technology's role in society.

What if I'm blacklisted by tech companies? Can I still apply?

Yes. In fact, that's often evidence of the ethical courage we're looking for.

"Blacklisting" usually means: "Refused to compromise and paid the price."

The Order doesn't care about your standing with corporations. We care about your technical competence and ethical track record.

Can I publish research and present at conferences?

Yes. Encouraged and supported.

The Order values:

  • Open-source contribution
  • Academic publication
  • Conference presentations
  • Industry standard-setting

Your work should influence the field, not be hidden.

What if a corporate job offers me more money later?

We hope you stay for the mission. But we're realistic.

If you receive a compelling offer:

  • No penalty for leaving (beyond losing future debt relief)
  • We'll provide references
  • Door remains open for potential return

We want people who want to be here.

How do I know this isn't a scam or will collapse in a year?

Legitimate concern. We provide:

Verification:

  • Legal entity documents (501c3 or equivalent)
  • Sponsoring institution letters of commitment
  • Financial transparency reports
  • Governance board member contacts

Protections:

  • Employment contracts include severance
  • Debt relief already provided is never reclaimed
  • Insurance coverage for operational continuity

Transparency:

  • Public quarterly reports
  • Open board meetings (observer access)
  • Financial statements available to applicants

We're building for decades, not months.

What's the catch?

The "catch": You must build excellent systems. Performance standards are high.

This is sanctuary for principled competence, not incompetence.

If you can't meet technical standards, don't actually prioritize ethics in practice, create conflict without cause, or underperform consistently—you won't last.

We're protecting you so you can do excellent work, not mediocre work.

Can I work remotely?

Depends on project and sponsor requirements.

  • Cybersecurity for specific institution: May require on-site presence
  • R&D and tool development: Often remote-friendly
  • Healthcare AI implementation: Hybrid likely

Discussed during application process based on your role and needs.

What if the Order loses funding or fails?

Risk mitigation:

  1. Diversified sponsorship: Multiple institutions, not single point of failure
  2. Endowment building: Year 1–3 focus on sustainability
  3. Contractual protections: Severance included in employment agreements
  4. Debt relief guarantee: Any payments made are never reclaimed

Worst case: You received debt relief + salary + meaningful work for however long the Order lasted. You're still better off than staying in corporate environment that fired you for ethics.

General Questions

How is the Order different from existing AI ethics initiatives?

Most initiatives are:

  • Academic: Research without implementation
  • Corporate: Ethics teams with no authority
  • Governmental: Policy without technical capability

The Order is:

  • Operational: Actually building systems
  • Protected: Institutional backing prevents corporate pressure
  • Technical: Staffed by experienced builders

We don't just study ethical AI. We build it.

What's the long-term vision?

Years 1–5: Proof of concept

  • Build core systems for sponsor institutions
  • Demonstrate model works
  • Refine governance and operations

Years 6–10: Expansion and replication

  • License systems to external organizations
  • Revenue generation toward sustainability
  • Additional cohorts and specializations
  • Model replication in other regions

Years 11+: Institutional permanence

  • Financial self-sustainability
  • Industry standard-setting authority
  • Global network of ethical technologists
  • Influence on AI development worldwide

Ultimate goal: Make ethical AI infrastructure so robust that the Order becomes obsolete because the principles are universally implemented.

Who's behind this?

Current status: Founding team includes technologists with ethical track records, faith institution representatives, academic advisors, and legal and policy experts.

Governance board forming with representation from sponsoring institutions and technical advisors.

All backgrounds verifiable and public.
