The Foundation: Rome Call for AI Ethics

What Is the Rome Call?

In 2020, the Vatican launched the "Rome Call for AI Ethics"—a global initiative to establish ethical principles for artificial intelligence development and deployment.

Signatories Include:

  • The Holy See (Vatican)
  • Microsoft
  • IBM
  • Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)
  • Italian Government
  • Multiple faith institutions and NGOs

The Goal: Create a shared ethical framework transcending religious, political, and corporate boundaries.

The Six Principles

1. Transparency

AI systems must be explainable and understandable. Black-box algorithms that make decisions without human comprehension are unacceptable.

Technical Translation: Explainable AI (XAI) must be standard. Every automated decision should be auditable.

2. Inclusion

AI must serve all people, especially the vulnerable and marginalized. Technology cannot be designed only for the privileged.

Technical Translation: Bias detection and correction must be built into design. Accessibility is mandatory, not optional.

3. Responsibility

Those who design, deploy, and profit from AI must be accountable for its effects. Automation does not eliminate responsibility.

Technical Translation: Human-in-the-loop oversight required. No fully autonomous decision-making in high-stakes contexts.

4. Impartiality

AI must not create or reinforce bias and discrimination. Fairness must be quantified and verified.

Technical Translation: Regular audits for demographic bias, continuous testing, public transparency reports.

5. Reliability

AI systems must function safely and dependably. Unreliable systems cannot be deployed at scale.

Technical Translation: Rigorous testing protocols, fail-safe mechanisms, continuous monitoring, incident reporting.

6. Security and Privacy

AI must protect user data and resist malicious attacks. Privacy is a right, not a commodity.

Technical Translation: Privacy-by-design architecture, encryption standards, minimal data collection, user control.

Why This Matters

The Rome Call achieved something unprecedented: Global consensus across competing interests.

  • Corporations agreed to ethical constraints
  • Governments committed to policy alignment
  • Faith traditions found common ground
  • NGOs validated comprehensive approach

The Achievement: Everyone agrees on the principles.

The Problem: No one has built the infrastructure to implement them.

The November 2025 Vatican Conference

"Artificial Intelligence and Medicine: Ethics and Governance"

Participants:

  • Catholic Church leadership
  • LDS/Mormon representatives
  • Orthodox Christian institutions
  • Protestant denominations
  • Medical professionals
  • AI researchers
  • Policy experts

Outcomes:

  1. Reaffirmed commitment to Rome Call principles
  2. Identified specific concerns in healthcare AI
  3. Demonstrated ecumenical collaboration already exists
  4. Recognized need for operational infrastructure

What Was Missing: A mechanism to turn dialogue into implementation.

That's what the Order provides.
